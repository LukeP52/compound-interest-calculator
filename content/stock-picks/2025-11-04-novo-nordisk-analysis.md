Novo Nordisk A/S ($NVO) is a Danish multinational pharmaceutical company specializing in diabetes care, obesity treatments, and other chronic diseases like hemophilia and growth disorders. Founded in 1923 through the merger of two Danish insulin producers, it has grown into a global leader in endocrinology, with a dominant position in insulin (about **46% global market share**) and GLP-1 receptor agonists.

Key products include **Ozempic and Rybelsus** for type 2 diabetes, **Wegovy** for weight management, and **NovoSeven** for hemophilia. The company operates in over 170 countries, employs around 63,000 people, and invests heavily in R&D, with a pipeline focusing on next-generation obesity drugs like CagriSema and oral semaglutide variants.

## Business Structure & Market Position

Novo Nordisk is dual-listed on the Copenhagen Stock Exchange (NOVO B) and as American Depositary Receipts (ADRs) on the NYSE, emphasizing sustainability and patient-centric innovation in its mission to "defeat diabetes and other serious chronic diseases."

The firm's business is segmented into:
- **Diabetes and Obesity Care**: ~85% of sales
- **Rare Disease**: Hemophilia and growth disorders
- **Other areas**: Emerging therapeutic categories

North America and International Operations drive revenue, with manufacturing facilities worldwide, including major expansions in Denmark and the U.S. to meet surging demand for GLP-1 drugs. As a pioneer in biotech, Novo Nordisk benefits from strong intellectual property, with **patents on semaglutide extending into the 2030s** in key markets.
