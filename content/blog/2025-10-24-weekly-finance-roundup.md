Welcome back to our weekly dive into the finance world. This past week, from October 20 to 24, 2025, saw markets grappling with persistent uncertainties from the ongoing U.S. government shutdown, while earnings optimism and Fed commentary provided some uplift. 

Stocks closed higher amid tech gains and easing bank fears, but the data blackout added fog to economic outlooks. Geopolitical tensions and global indicators also influenced sentiment. Let's break down the major happenings and their implications for investors.

## **Stock Market Rally: Earnings Optimism Fuels Gains Amid Volatility**

Wall Street ended sharply higher on October 20, with the Dow, S&P 500, and Nasdaq all advancing as finance and technology sectors led the charge. Apple hit an all-time high, boosting confidence, while gold recovered ground despite the prolonged shutdown. 

By week's end, U.S. stocks jumped overall, driven by upbeat quarterly earnings and reduced concerns over regional bank credit quality. Volume reached **17.50 billion shares** on October 20, below the recent average, reflecting cautious trading.

The municipal market maintained strength with only four trading days, buoyed by October's positive momentum. Defense stocks like Northrop Grumman, down slightly since the shutdown began but up **28% year-to-date**, outperformed the S&P 500's 15% gain. 

Rare earth stocks also drew attention, with analysts highlighting upside from government investments in companies like MP Materials. Earnings season built momentum, with S&P 500 Q3 growth expected at **9.3% year-over-year**, up from prior estimates.

## **Federal Reserve Commentary: Caution on Easing as Shutdown Clouds Data**

Federal Reserve Governor Christopher Waller urged caution against overly aggressive rate easing, supporting a 25-basis-point cut at the upcoming FOMC but tempering expectations for deeper moves. This commentary shifted sentiment, as markets had been pricing in more dovish actions. 

The ongoing government shutdown, entering its 20th day by October 20, created a data blackout, forcing investors to rely on Fed signals and headlines. White House advisor Kevin Hassett indicated the shutdown might end soon, providing some relief.

Economic projections remained steady, with the U.S. economy expected to grow **1.8% year-over-year** in 2025. Volatility spiked mid-month but left the rally largely intact, with markets eyeing upcoming CPI data for clarity.

## **Government Shutdown Persists: Economic and Market Ripples**

The federal shutdown continued into its third week, halting key data releases and amplifying uncertainty. This absence of official figures left policymakers and investors navigating in the dark, with private sources like PMI surveys gaining prominence. 

Impacts included potential delays in programs and broader economic drag, though markets showed resilience. Analysts noted the shutdown's role in subdued trading and bond market signals of waning confidence.

## **Global Developments: Inflation Data and Geopolitical Tensions**

Internationally, the week featured key inflation releases from New Zealand, Canada, and the UK, alongside PMI data offering insights into global trends. China's Q3 GDP and October PMI were closely watched, amid ongoing U.S.-China trade tensions. 

Japan hosted "Japan Weeks 2025," focusing on international finance from October 20-24. In Asia, stocks rose on U.S. gains, while China's factory activity contracted for a seventh month due to U.S. tariffs.

Crypto markets saw volatility, with Bitcoin around **$108,000-110,000** and Ethereum near **$3,900**, influenced by regulatory news and AI integrations. DeFi perpetual trading volume hit **$1 trillion** in October, signaling sector growth.
